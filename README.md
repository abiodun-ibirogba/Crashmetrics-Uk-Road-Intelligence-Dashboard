# CrashMetrics — UK Road Safety Intelligence Dashboard

## Overview
An end-to-end Power BI analytics project analysing over **1 million UK road 
traffic accident records** spanning 2005–2010. The dashboard uncovers seasonal 
accident patterns, geographic risk concentration, road type vulnerabilities, 
and lighting condition impacts, providing actionable intelligence for road 
safety intervention and policy decisions.

## Tools Used
- Power BI (Dashboard & Visualisation)
- DAX (Measures & Calculations)
- Microsoft Excel (Data Preparation)
- UK Road Safety Dataset (Kaggle)

## Key Findings
- 📈 Accidents peak in **November (130,347)** and dip in **February (105,238)** driven by weather, daylight shifts, and holiday-season traffic
- 🏙️ **Urban areas** account for 667,882 accidents, that is nearly double Rural 
  areas (380,550)
- 🛣️ **Single carriageways** are the most accident-prone road type, 
  outpacing all other road types combined
- 💡 **73.06%** of accidents occur in daylight, reflecting traffic volume 
  exposure, not daylight risk
- 📉 Accidents declined **27% over six years** from 198,735 (2005) 
  to 144,419 (2010) — signalling improving road safety enforcement

## DAX Measures Built
- Total Accidents
- Total Fatalities
- Month-on-Month Accident Change
- Average Casualties Per Accident
- YTD Fatalities
- Weekend vs Weekday Accidents

## Dashboard Pages
1. **Executive Overview** — KPI cards, monthly trend, top accident states
2. **Cause & Condition Analysis** — lighting, weather, and cause breakdown
3. **Road & Location Intelligence** — map visual, road type, slicer filters

## Recommendation
Future road safety campaigns and infrastructure investment should prioritize 
**urban single-carriageway roads**, where the concentration of risk is highest, 
while maintaining the downward momentum seen in the year-on-year accident trend.
